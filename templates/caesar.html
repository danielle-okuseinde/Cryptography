The Caesar Cipher

This is the Caesar cipher. It is a monoalphabetic cipher that shifts the alphabet by a shift factor and replaces each letter in the plaintext with the newly mapped letter. The formula for this cipher is E(x) = (x + n) mod 26, where x is the letter numerical value and n is the shift factor. Remember that the alphabet is numbered starting from 0 (A=0, Z=25).
